## Calculating Lactate Threshold Pace and HR

Well-trained runners: T-pace = ~85–88% of VO₂max
Lesser-trained runners: T-pace = ~80–86% of VO₂max

In practical terms, T-pace is the pace you could sustain race-hard for roughly 60 minutes if fully rested and peaked. It feels "comfortably hard" — manageable but something you look forward to finishing.
The easiest way to find your T-pace is to look it up in the VDOT tables using a recent race result (e.g. a 5K or 10K time).

Lactate Threshold Heart Rate (T-HR)
The book gives this as a percentage of your maximum heart rate (HRmax):

Well-trained runners: T-HR = ~88–92% of HRmax
Lesser-trained runners: T-HR = ~85–89% of HRmax

So the formula is simply:

T-HR = HRmax × 0.89 to 0.92 (well-trained)
T-HR = HRmax × 0.85 to 0.88 (lesser-trained)
